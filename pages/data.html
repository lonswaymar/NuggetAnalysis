Data Preparation and Cleaning

One place to begin understanding NBA data is with the three main statistics that our two featured athletes are most known for: points, rebounds, and assists.

  • Points occur when a player scores by shooting into the basket. They can be 1, 2, or 3 points depending on how the shot is made.
  • Assists occur when a player passes the ball to a teammate who scores immediately afterward.
  • Rebounds occur when a player grabs the ball after a missed shot — offensive or defensive.

A "triple-double" is achieved when a player records 10 or more in three different stat categories (typically PTS, AST, REB) in a single game. Westbrook and Jokic are among the all-time leaders in this category.

After cleaning, the dataset focuses only on relevant fields for triple-double tracking:

  • SEASON_ID: Season in which stats were recorded.
  • TEAM_ABBREVIATION: Short name of the team the player was with.
  • MIN: Total minutes played in the season.

Special handling: If a row had "TOT" (for total) in the team column, it was replaced with the team the player started the season with, based on the previous row.
